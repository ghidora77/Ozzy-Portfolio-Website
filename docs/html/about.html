My Work

I was going through my papers from college and graduate school, and realized that I had 179 of them - essays, long-form, history, scientific, philsophical, etc. Some could probably be trashed, some aren't worth publishing, but I hope that some of them are enjoyable and insightful. Rather than just let them sit in electronic folders, I figured I'd go through them all, one-by-one, clean them up, and start finding a way to connect the dots. For instance, one paper documents the evolution of the Iranian military since the Revolution - how can I expand on that? Are there connections between the rise of the Muslim Brotherhood in Syria, American technology development in the 1980's and demographic changes in Israel? We live in a global, hyper-connected world, where developments in one arena affect many other developments, oftentimes unseen.

My current research is in Remote Sensing and Geospatial analysis, using data analysis and machine learning techniques to glean insights from imagery. As a simple explanation, given a satellige image, a human is pretty good at identifying an aircraft or a mine or a road. But what that human is not good at is looking through a hundred billion of those images and craft a cohesive assessment of what is going on. There are techniques to 'train' a computer using machine learning to identify certain objects, allowing the human to make detailed assessments about what is going on.
