The Craft of Writing

Writing makes you think - what do you want to communicate, what point are you trying to make, who are you communicating with? The act of writing hones mastery, and I believe in communicating information that is clear, concise and readable. To that end, I hope to provide a distraction-free environment to interact with the content. While there is an abundance of information in textbooks, newspapers, the internet and in scientific journals, it can be overwhelming to read complex, complicated documents that are more about the writer's ego than the reader, or navigate websites that are covered with ads and distracting gimmicks. I hope you find these articles fun to read, but most importantly, enlightening.

There is relatively broad range of topics in the articles below, ranging from business analysis to international relations to essays in philosophy, but all have a common theme; using analytical thinking to understand and improve the world around us. Feel free to read any of the papers, ask questions and let me know if you have any comments or suggestions. I've separated data and programming papers into the 'Data Science' tab, while this page falls more into the humanities category.

International Affairs

As a society, we need to use the tools at our disposal to improve the global environment. After several years working on complex, cross-cultural issues in the military, I've found that the same type of analytical thinking in data and computer science can be applied to international affairs, which has traditionally been driven by instinct and cherry-picked information. I approach the subject by finding accurate data and sound reasoning to improve decision-making within global affairs.
